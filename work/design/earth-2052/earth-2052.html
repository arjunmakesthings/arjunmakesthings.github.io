earth 2052: hot or not?

2022

lead designer,
xperimenters programme,
at science gallery bengaluru.

as part of the xperimenters-cohort at science gallery bengaluru, the four of us were asked to present a public engagement project at the annual youth symposium. that year, the symposium was titled ‘hot & bothered’, and aimed to engage young people around the world in the global climate-crisis discourse.

we chose to steer away from traditional ‘scientific’ enquiries, and wished to do something with crowdsourced qualitative data; partly inspired by the work of guerrilla science. so, we put up a small stall at a local event in bengaluru, and asked 200+ young adults to draw what they think the earth would look like, 30 years into the future, given the current climate-crisis. each drawing was then manually analysed, and the findings were shared via a presentation at the symposium.

the responses were extremely diverse — some were grim, while others were filled with ample hope. some believed the earth would turn into a grand ball of fire, while some placed their faith in billionaire-technology-moghul-saviours.
